The Master Sword

"The sword that seals the darkness -- that only the chosen knight can wield against the Calamity Ganon" - Deku Tree

Planning
The Master Sword is a one handed longsword from the game Legend of Zelda, Breath of the Wild. By measuring the dimensions of the blade in game and the measurements of a character holding it we can find the real world dimensions. By measuring these values I determined the blade to have a length of 38 inches and the wings of the guard to span 12 inches. A couple of notable modifications that I decided to make to the sword was to thin down the blade from its original thickness in the game to a much more reasonable thickness. I also wanted the sword to have a more cylindrical grip instead of its normal perfectly circular one. This is so that the wielder of the blade can actually "hold zero" or keep the blade in line with where they are slashing. A cylindrical grip allows the wielder to essentially always hold the grip and by extension the sword the same way.
    The weapon should...
  1. have a cylindrical grip for holding zero.
  2. have a blade length of 38 inches.
  3. be able to be swung around with the ease of a normal sword.

The Master Sword from it's appearance in Breath of the Wild



Modeling
The Master Sword's blade was fairly straight forward to model in Solidwords due to its simple shape. The most complicated part of the project was making the hilt of the sword due to some of its complex geometry. Thankfully after several "Zero thickness Geometry" errors I was able to successfully shape the hilt with only some minor changes to its original appearance. The main difference of the sword is that the width of the wings and the blade have been thinned down.

A Solidworks assembly of the Master Sword



Prototyping
With the modeling completed the sword was ready to be 3D printed. After printing the blade as multiple pieces since the sword itself is too big to be printed as one piece it was time for the assembly. Assembling the blade unfortunatly led to an unfortunate result. While the blade looked great the inconsistent structure and the use of multiple parts made the blade very unstable, especially when swinging it. After sanding and priming it was clear that the 3D printed version of this blade would be much too unstable to be used. So where did this leave me?

With 3D printing not holding up as to the desired standards due to the thickness of the blade I had to turn to an alterior method for fabricating the blade. In this case, I decided to use the already 3D printed blade and cast the blade using resin. To do this I made some silicone molds out of the 3D printed blade. From here it was just a process of casting the blade to get the structure of the sword. From there I could then move to the finishing process for the blade.
